Bucharest JUG

Welcome!

Bucharest JUG is a friendly community of passionate developers who use one of the languages that are based on the JVM (Java Virtual Machine) - Java, Scala, Groovy, Clojure, JRuby.

Our regular monthly meetups begin around 7PM, feature slide presentations and provide opportunities for professional networking. Later on we go to a pub for drinks.

Say hello, stay in touch

Interested in being a speaker? Please e-mail us an abstract of your talk at hello@bjug.ro.

Interested in hosting a meetup? Please send a note to hello@bjug.ro.

About us

The Bucharest Java Users Group (BJUG) is a technical community that welcomes Java developers as well as software engineers who have an interest in any aspect or language surrounding the JVM platform.

Our objective is to build a strong community. Through our regular monthly meetings and web site, we aim to bring together members of Bucharest's Java community so they can share their tips, techniques, knowledge and experience.

Please let us know if you have any suggestions for our Java user group or the web site.

Brief history

The Bucharest Java Users Group was founded in 2012 by Andrei Savu, Ioan Eugen Stan and Mihai Soloi. The first meetup was hosted by Ceata with an audience of around 25 people. We have been growing ever since.

Meetup format

Meetups are conducted following a well-established, time-tested format.
They are typically scheduled on the last (or second-to-last) Thursday of every month. They begin around 19:00 and typically last one and a half hours in the formal setting. Afterwards, some participants go to a pub for drinks.

19:00~19:10 : Community announcements and a brief message from the sponsor
19:10~19:40 : Tech talk #1
19:40~19:55 : Networking break
19:55~20:25 : Tech talk #2
20:30~..... Participants leave; the group moves to a pub

Planning / organizing / sponsoring a meetup

The BJUG organizers and the sponsor make arrangements one month prior to every meeting. An announcement is subsequently placed on the front page of the web site along with date and location specifics as well as details of the talks. A copy of the announcement is sent to the mailing list.
Participants then sign up for the event using Eventbrite.
Our sponsor agrees to provide a conference room with a projector / screen and a capacity of at least 50 seats. At the beginning of the meetup, we invite our sponsor to give a 5-minute promotional presentation to the audience.
